Project Proposal Report · August 2025 · RP_25_26J_393

AI-Driven Demand Forecasting & Waste Management System for Sri Lankan Restaurants

An integrated AI platform that predicts menu demand, converts forecasts into ingredient purchases, and optimizes inventory buffers using wastage data, climate signals, and local contextual factors.

Explore Research View Documents

Domain Overview

Background & Literature Survey

Small-to medium-sized restaurants in Sri Lanka face chronic operational inefficiencies driven by unpredictable demand, seasonal price volatility, and limited access to data-driven tools. Manual estimation, ad-hoc supplier relationships, and no structured waste tracking result in stock-outs, over-purchasing, and significant food waste — challenges that established solutions from large supply-chain contexts cannot directly address.

Literature Review Highlights

01

Time-Series Forecasting

ARIMA and exponential smoothing are well-established, but fail to capture nonlinear demand patterns and Sri Lanka–specific cultural surges.

02

Machine Learning Approaches

Random Forest, LightGBM, and LSTMs outperform classical methods but mostly assume large, clean historical data.

03

Supplier Recommendation Systems

MCDM with AHP and ML-based ranking evaluate suppliers, primarily in large supply chains.

04

Privacy-Preserving Forecasting

Federated Learning enables collaborative model training without sharing raw sales data.

05

Waste Management & Inventory

EOQ and JIT models optimize static inventory but cannot adapt to perishable, demand-volatile restaurant environments.

06

External Variable Integration

Weather data, cultural calendars, and temporal features significantly improve demand forecast accuracy.

Research Problem

How can an integrated system be created that forecasts restaurant menu demand, anticipates ingredient price fluctuations, recommends reliable suppliers, and reduces food waste — specifically for Sri Lankan restaurants operating with limited structured data, low digital infrastructure, and privacy-sensitive supply relationships?

Research Objectives

  • Develop a hybrid ML + rule-based demand forecasting model tailored for data-scarce Sri Lankan SMEs.
  • Integrate cultural calendar and weather data to capture local demand surges accurately.
  • Build an adaptive inventory buffer driven by actual kitchen waste via exponential smoothing.
  • Predict weekly and monthly food selling prices using ARIMA, Prophet, Regression, and LSTM models.
  • Implement a supplier ranking and recommendation engine based on cost, reliability, and freshness.
  • Design a privacy-preserving supplier demand forecasting dashboard using federated learning.
  • Validate accuracy using MAE, RMSE, R², and MAPE against baseline methods.

System Components

Each component is independently developed and seamlessly integrated into a unified decision-support pipeline for Sri Lankan restaurants and their suppliers.

Component 01

Lightweight Hybrid Food Demand Forecaster

Thilakarathna W.P.N.S. · IT22073846

The foundational forecasting engine. Combines a lightweight ML core with a weighted fuzzy-logic contextual engine to produce context-aware daily and weekly demand predictions.

The ML core uses LightGBM and Facebook Prophet trained on historical sales augmented with lag and rolling averages. The contextual engine overlays real-time OpenWeatherMap meteorological data and a standardised Sri Lankan cultural calendar.

Component 02

AI-Enabled Inventory & Reorder Management System

Jayathunga A.G.I.A. · IT22642950

Transforms demand forecasts into physical inventory actions. Computes total ingredient requirements through recipe mapping and features a pioneering Adaptive Buffer Algorithm.

The adaptive buffer treats kitchen waste as an active data signal rather than a passive metric. By tracking daily unsold food and applying exponential smoothing, the system autonomously corrects safety buffers without human intervention.

Component 03

Food Price Prediction & Supplier Recommendation

Fernando W.G.P.N · IT22261946

Food Price Prediction Model

Using historical food selling prices, ingredient cost data, and current market prices, the component forecasts weekly and monthly dish selling prices. Models benchmarked include ARIMA, Prophet, Regression, and LSTM.

Supplier Recommendation Engine

A multi-criteria ranking algorithm scores each supplier on cost efficiency, delivery reliability, and freshness ratings. AHP-based MCDM ensures transparent, explainable recommendations.

  • ✔️ Weekly and monthly dish price predictions
  • ✔️ Best supplier ranked per ingredient
  • ✔️ Price-spike alerts via WhatsApp
  • ✔️ AHP / MCDM Decision-support report generation
  • ✔️ PDF and Excel export for business use
Component 04

Supplier Demand Forecasting Dashboard

Alawaththa A.K.A.A · IT22249470

Forecasting Models

Collects dish-level sales data from restaurants, converts to ingredient-level demand, and applies an ensemble of ARIMA, Holt-Winters, and Random Forest Regression models.

Privacy-Preserving Aggregation

Using Federated Learning (FL), each restaurant trains its local model and shares only model parameters — never raw transactional data. Optional Differential Privacy layer adds controlled noise to protect identity.

Inventory Optimisation Logic

Supplier-facing reorder points are computed using Economic Order Quantity (EOQ). The interactive dashboard provides time-series graphs, inventory heatmaps, and stock alerts.
